FOOD & BEVERAGES

DANISH PASTRIES


The concept was brought to Denmark by Austrian bakers, where the recipe was partly changed and accommodated by the Danes to their liking, and has since developed into a Danish specialty.You can eat it with fruit and cream cheese.

2 / 5

OYSTER


Denmark is home to two types of oysters, The first is the Limfjord oyster and second oyster is the Pacific oyster (crassostrea gigas)

3 / 5

RISALAMANDE

The most popular Danish Christmas dessert, Risalamande is a wonderfully creamy almond rice pudding that is served topped with a delicious cherry sauce!

4 / 5

HYLDEBLOMSTSAFT


An elderflower juice mixed with water. It is usually served cold, but you might try it served hot in winter.

5 / 5

AKVAVIT


Akvavit called snaps as well is a high proof spirit made from potatoes and flavoured with a variety of herb and make sure you try it in the Christmas season.
